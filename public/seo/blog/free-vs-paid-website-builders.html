Free vs Paid Website Builders: What's Best for Local Businesses

March 26, 2026 8 min read

If you're a local business owner looking to get a website, the number of options can be overwhelming. Wix, Squarespace, WordPress, GoDaddy, Weebly, AI builders — each one claims to be the best. So which one actually is?

The honest answer: it depends on your situation. But most local businesses are paying too much for features they don't need. Let's break down the real costs and trade-offs.

The Quick Comparison

Platform Monthly Cost Setup Time Technical Skill Best For
Wix (Free) $0 2-4 hours Low Hobby sites
Wix (Paid) $17-35 2-4 hours Low Small businesses wanting drag-and-drop
Squarespace $16-49 3-6 hours Low-Medium Design-focused businesses
WordPress.com $4-45 4-10 hours Medium Content-heavy sites, blogs
WordPress (self-hosted) $5-30 + hosting 1-3 days High Full control, complex sites
AI-Generated $0-20 5-15 minutes None Local businesses wanting speed

The "Free" Trap

Let's get this out of the way: free website builders aren't really free. Here's what you give up:

  • Your domain: Your URL will be something like yourbusiness.wixsite.com instead of yourbusiness.com. This looks unprofessional and hurts your Google ranking.
  • Their branding: A "Made with Wix" banner on your site tells customers you didn't invest in your online presence.
  • Limited features: No e-commerce, no custom forms, limited storage, no analytics.
  • Slower speeds: Free plans typically run on shared, slower servers — which kills your Google ranking.
Verdict: Free plans are fine for personal projects or testing. For a real business, they do more harm than good. The unprofessional appearance and subdomain can actively turn away customers.

Wix and Squarespace: The Drag-and-Drop Standards

These are the go-to choices for most small businesses, and they're genuinely good products. But here's what people don't tell you:

The Hidden Costs

  • $17-49/month for the platform itself
  • $12-20/year for a custom domain (often "free" the first year, then you pay)
  • $0-15/month for premium apps/plugins you'll probably want
  • 4-10 hours of your time building and tweaking the site

That's $200-600 per year plus your time. For a local barbershop or taco stand, that's real money.

The Time Factor

This is the part that catches people off guard. "Drag and drop" sounds easy, but actually building a professional-looking website takes time. You need to:

  • Choose a template (there are hundreds — decision paralysis is real)
  • Customize colors, fonts, and layout
  • Write all the copy for every page
  • Source and optimize images
  • Set up your contact form, hours, and map
  • Make sure it looks good on mobile (it often doesn't by default)
  • Configure SEO settings

Most business owners start on a Saturday afternoon and either finish something they're not happy with or abandon the project entirely.

Verdict: Wix and Squarespace are solid choices if you have the time, enjoy design work, and need ongoing content management (like a blog or store). For a simple business presence, they're often overkill.

WordPress: Powerful but Complex

WordPress powers 43% of all websites on the internet. It's incredibly capable — but that capability comes at a cost.

  • Self-hosted WordPress gives you total control but requires technical knowledge: hosting, security, updates, backups, plugin conflicts, etc.
  • WordPress.com is simpler but more expensive and more limited than Wix/Squarespace
  • Themes and plugins can conflict, break, or become security vulnerabilities
  • You'll likely need professional help at some point, which adds cost
Verdict: WordPress is best for businesses that need complex functionality (membership sites, e-commerce with 100+ products, multi-author blogs). For a local business that needs a solid online presence, it's usually more complexity than it's worth.

AI-Generated Websites: The New Option

This is the category that didn't exist two years ago, and it's changing the game for local businesses. Here's how it works:

  1. You provide your business name, type, and basic info
  2. AI generates a complete, professional website in minutes
  3. The site includes proper SEO, mobile optimization, and fast loading by default
  4. You review it and request any changes

What AI Gets Right

  • Speed: A complete site in minutes instead of hours or days
  • No learning curve: You don't need to learn any tool or interface
  • Professional copy: AI writes compelling, relevant content about your business
  • Built-in best practices: SEO, mobile responsiveness, and performance are handled automatically
  • Low cost: No monthly subscriptions eating into your margins

What AI Doesn't Do (Yet)

  • Complex e-commerce (if you're selling 50+ products, you still need Shopify)
  • Ongoing content management (if you're publishing blog posts weekly)
  • Highly custom interactive features (booking systems, member portals)
Verdict: For the majority of local businesses — restaurants, salons, clinics, contractors, shops — an AI-generated website gives you 90% of what you need at a fraction of the cost and time. It's the fastest path from "no website" to "professional online presence."

So What Should You Choose?

Here's a simple decision framework:

  • You need a site NOW and have no technical skills: AI-generated website
  • You enjoy design and want hands-on control: Wix or Squarespace
  • You're selling lots of products online: Shopify
  • You need complex functionality and have technical support: WordPress
  • You're on a tight budget: AI-generated website

The most important thing isn't which platform you choose — it's that you actually get a website online. A good-enough site that exists will always outperform a perfect site that's still "in progress."
